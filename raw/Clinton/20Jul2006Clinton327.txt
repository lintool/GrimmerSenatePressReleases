July 20  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Backs  83 4 Million for Buffalo Courthouse                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Senators Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill      Courthouse is Essential to Development of Historic Downtown District                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee is expected to approve later today  83 4 million for the Buffalo Federal Courthouse under the FY07 Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development  Schumer and Clinton had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill            In March  when my amendment got this added to the budget I knew we were on track  but now funding seems much more certain     Schumer said    This new courthouse will be a lynchpin of Downtown Buffalo  For far too long the Courthouse has been on the back burner and I have worked to see it finally get the recognition from Congress that it deserves  The Federal Courthouse is vitally important to the economic growth and revitalization of downtown Buffalo               This is incredible news   but it  s about time  This is one of the biggest pieces in the City of Buffalo  s plan to revitalize downtown  We have all been excited about this project for a long time but thanks to the Bush Administration  it has been continually put on hold  I am thrilled to have fought for this funding and thrilled that it has paid off  I will also be working to fully authorize the project next week in the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee  If the Administration won  t help  we will make sure that Congress makes good on this promise to Buffalo     Senator Clinton said          Buffalo  s Federal Courthouse  which is to be the centerpiece of downtown development  received  83 4 million of federal funding to begin construction of a new building  Construction was set to begin at Niagara Square and Delaware Avenue in 2002  but was delayed due to lack of funding  Breaking through the federal bureaucracy that was keeping the dollars for this project from getting freed up was essential to getting this project off the ground  Today  s funds signal the end of that inertia and now the ball is rolling on this key project  The Federal court is currently located in a building constructed in 1936 as part of a Public Works project and is sorely lacking in space and modern security measures  The new facility would alleviate the space shortages at the current facility by including new office space for judges and magistrates  the US attorney and the US Marshal s Service  In addition  the new building would provide grand jury space and prisoner holding cells  Centrally located in the downtown area  the Courthouse will anchor the ongoing downtown revitalization effort           Later today  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
