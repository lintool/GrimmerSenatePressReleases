March 7  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  3 5 Million In Federal Money For Syracuse Hancock International Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Federal Funding Included in FY05 Omnibus Appropriations bill    Airport Improvement Grant Will Rehabilitate Airfield Drainage  Improve Surface Water Runoff  and Improve Safety at Syracuse Hancock International Airport                                                                                                                                                                                US Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that Syracuse Hancock International Airport has been awarded over  3 5 million in federal funds for safety enhancements at the airport   The funds specifically will be used to rehabilitate airfield drainage and measure and improve surface water runoff   The funds are included in the Transportation  Treasury  and Independent Agencies Appropriations section of the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill          This is great news for Central New York   said Schumer   The Syracuse Hancock International Airport welcomes travelers to New York each year  and these improvements are important for strengthening the local economy  I will continue to fight for federal funds to help the airport remain a thriving center of activity and a hub for economic growth in the region           Syracuse Hancock International Airport is an integral part of the local economy and it is important that we do everything we can to support it   Senator Clinton said   Any opportunity we have to improve access to the airport is critical  because more flights and better service simply means more jobs and more opportunities for the region
