July 20  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Backs  2 Million for Yonkers Economic Development                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senators Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill      Ashburton Avenue Project Key Step In Downtown Revitalization in Yonkers  NY                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee is expected to approve later today  2 000 000 for the Ashburton Avenue Reconstruction project under the FY07 Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development   Schumer and Clinton had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill            This is great news for the city of Yonkers     Schumer said    Improving access to the area is a key step in revitalization of the Waterfront area  This project is one small step in a larger project that will bring new residents  businesses  and visitors to downtown Yonkers  This project will breathe new life to the district and provide a shot in the arm for the local economy               Yonkers is growing at a rapid rate and the city is taking advantage of this growth with a concerted and coordinated effort to revitalize one of its greatest assets    the waterfront  These funds will be a great boost to the City of Yonkers   revitalization efforts  with the promise of a great future for the City and residents alike     Senator Clinton said          Funding will go towards improving access to the rapidly growing waterfront area of Yonkers  NY  Over the past decade  the Yonkers Office of Downtown and Waterfront Development has overseen the revitalization and productive utilization of the four and a half miles of waterfront along the Hudson River in Yonkers  New projects including additional residential rental units  office space  marinas  parks and retail centers are being planned and constructed  The common thread in this blending of mixed usages is public access to the river for relaxation  recreation and education for the citizens of Yonkers and visitors to the area  Funding for this particular project will support new residential development by widening Ashburton Avenue from Yonkers Avenue to Alexander Street and facilitating easier access to the area           Later today  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
