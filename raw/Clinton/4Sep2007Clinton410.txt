September 4  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  937 000 to Plattsburgh International Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Federal Money Will Be Used to Acquire Aircraft Rescue and Fire Fighting Vehicle  as well as Conduct Wildlife Hazard Assessment Study                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has awarded two grants totaling  937 175 in Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  funds to Plattsburgh International in Clinton County   The first grant  totaling  900 600 will be used to acquire an aircraft rescue and fire fighting vehicle  The second grant  totaling  36 575 will be used to conduct a wildlife hazard assessment study    This is great news for Clinton County  said Schumer  Plattsburgh International is an important economic engine in the North Country    It is critical that we continue to provide the funds necessary to keep the airport safe and efficient   With so many local aviators  tourists and businesspeople using the airport as a base of operation  it remains a key component of economic growth in the region     This is welcome news for Clinton County  said Senator Clinton   Both of these grants will go a long way to help ensure the Plattsburgh International Airport remains safe and secure  I am pleased to support this effort that will help the airport remain critical to the local economy    The first grant  totaling  900 600 will be used to acquire an aircraft rescue and fire fighting vehicle  The second grant  totaling  36 575  will be used to conduct a wildlife hazard assessment study  These projects will better position the airport to serve the surrounding business community more effectively  efficiently and safely
