November 6  2007 Schumer  Clinton   300 000 for SUNY New Paltz Included in Final Health and Human Services Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Federal Funding Will Be Used for Curriculum Development   Schumer  Clinton Urged Committee to Include Funding In Spending Bill   Bill will now be sent back to Senate for Final Approval                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington  DC   Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that a House Senate Conference Committee has agreed on a final version of the Labor  Health and Human Services and Education FY08 Appropriations Bill  which includes  300 000 for SUNY New Paltz  The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature  Schumer and Clinton urged the committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill    This is great news for the Hudson Valley and another critical step in the appropriations process  said Schumer  This project will help SUNY New Paltz create a stronger and more specialized academic program for our students  providing them with the absolute best in higher education  I will work hard to continue to secure funding for these critical programs    I welcome the news that the conference committee has taken the next step in securing these funds for SUNY New Paltz and the Hudson Valley  said Senator Clinton   This is an investment in the future that will allow SUNY New Paltz to continue providing its students a world class curriculum    SUNY New Paltz will use  300 000 in federal funds for curriculum development in economic development and governance    The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature
