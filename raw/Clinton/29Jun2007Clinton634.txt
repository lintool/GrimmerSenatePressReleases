June 29  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  500 000 for the Onondaga County Interoperable Communciation System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Federal Funds Will Be Used to Upgrade Equipment to Improve Communication System for First Responders   Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the Commerce  Justice  Science  and Related Agencies FY08 Appropriations Bill which includes  500 000 for the Onondaga County Interoperable Communication System   The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor as the next step in the appropriations process   Schumer and Clinton worked closely with members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill    This is great news for Syracuse and Onondaga  said Schumer   These funds will be by used Onondaga County to unify and streamline the communication system for first responders into one network  making them it more efficient and effective   This system a crucial part of keeping our communities safe by ensuring that our first responders have the equipment and technology they need to coordinate their efforts when responding to emergencies     One of the most critical tools that law enforcement and first responders have is the ability to communicate and these funds will certainly help Onondaga County improve their interoperable communications systems  Our first responders must be equipped with the best available communications systems to effectively react and respond when they are needed  They put their lives on the line for us every day and we should give them the tools they need to keep us safe and to support our communities  I am very pleased that we can help the County is in this way  Senator Clinton said    Onondaga County will use federal fund to improve the Interoperable Communications System for first responders in Syracuse and the surrounding areas   Funds will be used to replace an incompatible 800MHz radio equipment to the proposed City County UHF Trunked Land Mobile Radio Interoperable Communication System  Infrastructure equipment currently used to support two way radio communications will not be consistent with equipment planned for the new first responder system that supports operations in the City of Syracuse and County of Onondaga    Onondaga County is in the process of building and planning to implement a trunked radio UHF Land Mobile Radio System  This system when implemented will embrace all first responders in the City of Syracuse and County of Onondaga   The planned system will comply with the all the standards outline in the SAFECOM continuum  consistent with the ICOM Act  and meet the Standards outlined in the 2006 Homeland Security Grant Program Guidelines  Federal programs that have advanced Interoperable Communications have been used as the standard for which this initiative has been undertaken and fully supported by all of the political leadership in both the City of Syracuse and County of Onondaga   This request is coordinated among several law enforcement entities through the formation of the Onondaga County Interoperable Communications System Committee   This project will consolidate all voice communications into one operational system     Infrastructure purchases would include  21 Channel Site Controller  Antenna System for LMR  Channel  Site Software  Channel Banks  and Channel Cards  The purchase of this equipment will be incorporated with the overall systems replacement planned for this area  Several Fire Departments in the County and City serving a population base of 250 000 will be affected by this change    Now that the bill has been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  it will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bill will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
