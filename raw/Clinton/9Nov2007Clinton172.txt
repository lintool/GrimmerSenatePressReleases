November 9  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Senate Approval of  4 4 Million In Defense Funding for New York s North Country                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Funds Included in Department of Defense Appropriations Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Washington  DC  Senators Charles Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Senate has approved the Fiscal Year 2008 Department of Defense Appropriations bill conference report which includes  4 4 million in defense funding for New Yorks North Country    The bill  having been approved by both chambers of Congress  will now be sent to the President for his signature    This is terrific news for the North Country and a vital step in the appropriations process  said Senator Schumer  These critical funds will help us ensure that in defending our nation  we are equipped with the most cutting edge technology and know how  All of the institutions awarded here are doing research and work that will boost both our nations defense forces and the North Countrys economy  I will urge the president to sign this bill immediately    This is excellent news not only for the North Country  but also for our men and women in uniform   The cutting edge research and development carried out by these local businesses and research institutions will help provide our troops with advanced military technology   At the same time  this funding is a great investment in the North Country economy   I applaud the conference committee for approving funds that will allow these important projects to continue  and I look forward to the President signing this bill into law  said Senator Clinton    The  4 4 million for New Yorks North Country in the Fiscal Year 2008 Department of Defense Appropriations bill includes   Clarkson University  Advancement of Intelligent Aerospace Systems  AIAS  for the U S  Air Force    2 million    Advancement of Intelligent Aerospace Systems  AIAS  will integrate flow control technology assessment with advanced weapons system technology assessments and will help to reduce the time and cost of developing and transitioning promising flow control technologies   The program scope for AIAS is development of simulation based flow control design tools that will enable aerospace systems for the new generation of Air Force advanced aerospace vehicles  and development of valuable simulation tools with software and hardware elements for assessment of proposed future Air Force weapon systems that incorporate flow control concepts   Legend Technologies  Remote Sighting System    800 000   Funding will help to finish development of a new optical technology to provide maximum possible effectiveness of the robotic warrior  force multipliers  and remote weapon platforms currently being fielded and developed  The final development and deployment of this technology will have an impact on reducing combat casualties among our servicemembers as well as providing for more effective offensive capabilities  Current optical platforms are insufficient to the current mission and  as a result  more troops are required to carry out certain dangerous combat missions  This re fit and new technology for robot warriors will help save lives    Trudeau Institute  U S  Navy Pandemic Influenza Vaccines Program    1 6 million   Funds will support research to understand mechanistically how immune responses are initiated and regulated   This information will facilitate the improvement of existing vaccines and promote the development of new vaccine strategies that elicit long lasting protective immunity against respiratory pathogens   Pandemic influenza would significantly affect military readiness   An outbreak would degrade combat mission capability among vulnerable troops  stress military medical operations to maximum capacity  restrict military operations  and divert military manpower for health care or crowd control   The ability of all military units to conduct their missions could be greatly reduced for an extended period of time
