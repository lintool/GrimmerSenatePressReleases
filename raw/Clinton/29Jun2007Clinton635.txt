June 29  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves Federal Funding For Projects in the Southern Tier                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Bill Includes Funding for the Susquehanna River Environmental Restoration and Low Flow Project  Cooperstown Upper Susquehanna River Basin Environmental Restoration Project  Chenango Lake  Sidney Lake  Whitney Point Lake  Arkport Dam   Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending Bill   Bill Will Now be Sent to the Senate Floor                             Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the Energy and Water Development FY08 Appropriations Bill which includes several Army Corps of Engineers projects in the Southern Region   The Senate bill contains  100 000 for the Susquehanna River Environmental Restoration and Low Flow project   102 000 for the Upper Susquehanna River Basin Envirnomental Restoration project in Cooperstown   671 000 for East Sydney Lake and  320 000 for Arkport Dam  Funding is also included for Aquatic Ecosystem Restoration at Chenango Lake and Environmental Improvements at Whitney Point Lake  but the amount is not yet determined   The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor as the next step in the appropriations process   Schumer and Clinton worked closely with members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill    This is great news for the Southern Tier  said Schumer   These projects are and important part of preserving the ecosystems of the regions many lakes and bodies of water   It is essential that we utilize these resources in a responsible way that meets the needs of the communities while also making an effort to maintain their beauty for years to come    This funding could not have come at a better time for the region as we continue to look at the best ways of maintaining our water sources while mitigating the possible impact of future flooding  If we are going to be prepared for heavy rains in the future it is critical that we maintain our dams  our river systems and our other essential infrastructure and these funds will go a long way to helping local communities with their financial and construction needs  They will also help us preserve our natural water sources for generations to come  Senator Clinton said    Susquehanna River Environmental Restoration and Low Flow received  100 000 in the Senate bill for work that will be performed by the Army Corps of Engineers  The Susquehanna River Basin drains an area exceeding 27 500 square miles  covering half the land area of Pennsylvania  and portions of New York and Maryland   While the basin is noted as one of the most flood prone in the United States  there is increasing concern over the growing demand for the water both in and out of the basin as the population of more than 4 1 million people continues to grow   Consumptive water use continues to increase throughout the basin with sector uses of power production  municipal supplies and agriculture sharing the highest demand   With the pressures of urban sprawl surrounding population centers  and increases in consumptive use  there is a need to address low flow issues and the increasing demand on the water resources of the basin   The opportunity exists now to address strategies to pool and release water to offset the growing demands and consumptive uses of water in the basin     Other projects included in the Senate bill for work performed by the Army Corps of Engineers in the Southern Tier Region  Now that the bill has been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  it will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bill will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
